About Tetris

The History of Tetris

Tetris was created in 1984 by Russian game designer Alexey Pajitnov. The name "Tetris" is derived from the Greek numerical prefix "tetra-" (meaning "four") and tennis, Pajitnov's favorite sport.

The game quickly became a phenomenon after its release and has since been ported to nearly every gaming platform in existence. Its simple yet addictive gameplay has made it one of the best-selling video games of all time.

How to Play

The goal of Tetris is to manipulate falling tetrominoes (geometric shapes composed of four squares) to create complete horizontal lines without gaps.

  • Rotate pieces to fit them together
  • Complete lines to clear them from the board
  • Score points for each line cleared
  • The game ends when pieces stack to the top
